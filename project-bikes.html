Bike Sales Profitability Audit
Half the dataset was fake. Proving it came first.

A 113,000-row sales dataset, audited entirely in Excel. Before any profitability work, a data-quality audit revealed that roughly half the rows were fabricated — and the analysis only proceeded once that was proven, flagged and isolated.

Excel Formula-driven Data-quality audit Self-directed · 2026
Download the audit workbook (Excel, 25 MB)

The problem

The brief looked simple: analyse profitability across 113K sales rows. But early exploration didn't add up — patterns repeated too neatly. Instead of pushing on, the project pivoted: is this data even real?

Proving the fabrication

  • The leap-day test — genuine multi-year transaction data must contain sales on 29 February. A suspect block of rows contained none, while the verified block did — a simple calendar fact the fabricator forgot to fake.
  • Row-matching — systematic comparison exposed suspect rows as near-duplicates of genuine ones with altered values, confirming they were generated, not recorded.
  • Flagged, never deleted — every fabricated row was marked with audit flags and excluded by formula, not removed. Anyone can re-run, inspect or challenge the audit. Auditability over convenience.
~50% of the dataset failed the audit. Every chart built on the full file would have been confidently, invisibly wrong.

The profitability findings

On verified data only, the margin story was sharp:

  • Discounting is the silent profit leak — margin erodes from 51% at full price to 31% at deep discounts. Each discount band buys volume with disproportionate margin.
  • Helmets out-earn bikes — at roughly 59% margin, the highest-ticket item isn't the most profitable one. Accessories quietly carry the economics.

Why it matters

Everything here is 100% formula-driven Excel — audit flags, the verified-data layer and an interactive dashboard, with no manual edits anywhere in the pipeline. It demonstrates two things at once: deep Excel craft, and the analyst instinct that matters most — validate before you analyse.
